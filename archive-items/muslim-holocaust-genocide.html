§ 01 · The Argument

Lying by omission is worse than lying by commission.

Polya's central contention: Western military interventions since 9/11 have caused mass Muslim death — most of it invisible, because most of it comes not from bombs but from the deprivation that follows them: shattered water, food, medicine, and public health.

Under the UN Genocide Convention, genocide includes not only killing, but “deliberately inflicting on the group conditions of life calculated to bring about its physical destruction.” Polya argues that sustained, remorseless action establishes intent — no formal declaration is required.

What follows is that argument rendered as data: the dead counted, the displaced mapped, the scale set against the great atrocities of the modern record. These are one scholar's estimates, presented faithfully and in full.

§ 09 · The Legal Standard

What the law actually says.

UN Convention on the Prevention & Punishment of Genocide · Article II
“…any of the following acts committed with intent to destroy, in whole or in part, a national, ethnical, racial or religious group…”
Killing members of the group
Causing serious bodily or mental harm
Deliberately inflicting conditions of life calculated to bring about physical destruction
Imposing measures intended to prevent births
Forcibly transferring children of the group

On these figures. The mortality numbers presented here are the documented estimates of Dr. Gideon Polya, derived from his avoidable-mortality methodology (UN demographic data, observed vs. expected deaths). They are larger than several other published counts and are the subject of scholarly debate. GSS presents them here as a faithful visualization of one rigorous, dissenting body of work — part of the anarchive's commitment to amplifying analyses silenced or omitted by mainstream accounts.
