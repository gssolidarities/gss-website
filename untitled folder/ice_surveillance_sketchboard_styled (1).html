FILE 05

The New Jim Code

Flock cameras have an inherent racial bias because they are produced using the same Artificial Intelligence that is already causing problems throughout the United States. These issues go back to issues as simple as sensors failing to recognize darker skin tones to the extremes of AI cameras seeing danger when there is a black or brown person setting up a deadly situation (Dykos and Pryor). The idea of racist technology circles back to "The New Jim Code".

◈ BENJAMIN (61) "...one tech company representative noted that, 'if the training data is produced by a racist society, it won't matter who is on the team, but the people who are affected should also be on the team.'"
◈ BENJAMIN (62) "...databases, just like courtrooms, banks, and emergency rooms, do not contain organic brains. Yet legal codes, financial practices, and medical care often produce deeply racist outcomes."

Explaining how we have come to the point that cameras interpret black children with chip bags as aggressors with guns. Racial bias is ingrained in the codes because racial bias is inherent in the people who write the codes. Without representation in tech-companies or as product testers there is no one around to point out these biases.

FILE 06

The Mountain View Case

ICE is using Flock cameras to detain people with a panopticon built by an amalgamation of people. Instead of relying on one person to install cameras to track people, cameras are installed and a back door is opened by the company for other agencies to use the data. California is often thought of as a liberal haven, but that is not the case.

A local newscast spread the information about Flock cameras in Mountain View, California being accessed by the Federal Government. The city of Mountain View who installed and paid for the cameras did not give permission for anyone outside city officials to access the cameras.

◈ FLOCK CEO — BRADBURY "Flock's own CEO reportedly acknowledged last August that the company had been running previously undisclosed pilot programs with Customs and Border Protection and Homeland Security Investigations."

There is no way to trust a surveillance company who promises to not keep information private. The government is using cameras paid for by other people — other law enforcement agencies — to surveil private citizens in hopes of finding people they view as criminals. When law enforcement agencies start turning on each other, private citizens begin to understand how disastrous Artificial Intelligence is to everyday people.
